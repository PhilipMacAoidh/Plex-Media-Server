  • Basics

  • How do I sign up?

    To signup visit netphilix.ie and wait for the login screen to appear. You can signup by loggining in with your Google or Facebook account. Otherwise you can signup by giving your email address and a password. After you have signed up you need to send the email address that you signed up with (the email address for your Google or Facebook account if you signed up that way) to admin@netphilix.ie. Once I recieve the email I will add you to the server asap. I will reply to your email when I have added you. When you recieve the email return to your plex account and go to 'Settings'->'Users'->'Friends', you should see an invite for you to join my server. Click on the green tick mark and you will then have full access to the server! Welcome, and enjoy! ;)

  • Can I request a movie or TV show?

    In the pane on the left side of the screen select the 'Plugins' section. Next you will need to select 'Request Channel', then select 'Request a Movie' or 'Request a TV Show'. You will be prompted to enter the name of the Movie/TVShow in the searchbox at the top of the screen and press enter, after you have searched, select the poster of the Movie/TVShow you want and select yes. Your TV Show or Movie is being downloaded!

  • Can I download a movie or TV show for offline viewing?

    The original Movie and TV Show files are availible to download from Netphilix.ie/download. Sometimes your browser will begin to play the video rather than dowload it. To download, just right click on the video that's playing and select 'Save video as...', this will begin the download.


    The application that I use for my server (Plex) will only allow downloading for offline viewing if you subscribe to the plex pass. More info here.

    If you subscribe to the plex pass, contact me at admin@netphilix.ie and I can show you how to use the offline viewing feature.

  • How do I use the podcasts section?

    Podcast Discovery Screen

    The main podcast page can be found in the web app on the bottom of the left sidebar under “Online Content”.



    In the iOS and Android mobile apps, it can be found via the navigation row at the bottom of screen. Depending on how you arranged this on mobile you, may have to hit the … More button.


    Related Page: Navigating the Apps



    In Roku, select Podcasts on the left navigation.



    On the main podcast screen in app, you may see:

    On Deck:

    Episodes for shows in “My Podcasts” that are in-progress or are up next and waiting

    Recommended:

    Recommendations based on your podcast listening (it’s a set of popular podcasts, if you have not listened to any yet)

    My Podcasts:

    Enter a list of all your saved podcasts

    Categories:

    A list of categories you can browse as desired




    My Podcasts: Adding and Removing


    Adding

    To track podcasts, you add/save that podcast to My Podcasts. On Deck will only show episodes from those saved in My Podcasts.


    There are a few ways to add/save podcasts to My Podcasts, depending on app.

    -Go to the main summary screen for that Podcast show and use the Add to My Podcasts button

    -In supported mobile apps, hit the … menu on an episode and choose to add

    -On the main Podcast screen, use the Add a Podcast by URL button to manually add a podcast (top right in the web app and at the bottom of the mobile apps)

    -Import OPML file exported from another podcasting app so you can add all your favorite podcasts at once. (In the Web app, after clicking on the Add a Podcast by URL button you will see option to import OPML)


    Tip!: Most websites for podcasts will have an RSS feed link that you can use.


    Removing


    You can remove a podcast from My Podcasts in several ways:

    -From an episode On Deck use the … menu and choose to remove from My Podcasts

    -On the preplay screen for that podcast in the web app, click the In My Podcasts button so that the green checkmark disappears

    -In mobile apps click the … menu and choose to remove




    Downloading Podcast to Plex mobile apps


    You can download individual episodes or a number of episodes per podcast show from a Plex Mobile app.


    For an individual podcast episode navigate to that episode in the app and click on the download arrow.


    To download a number of episodes for a Podcast show navigate to the main show page and click the download arrow. Then you will be presented with another screen with options for how many to keep at a time and when to delete an episode after it has been played.



    You can view download status and delete episodes manually in the apps Download and Sync settings



    Podcast Settings


    Each Podcast has a couple settings. You can access these via the preference menu on the podcast show preplay (not episode preplay).


    -Order by Newest or Oldest first

    -Change playback speed default for that show from 0.5x to 2x

  • What are Collections?

    Finding the Collections within a library


    Collections can be discovered or viewed in four main ways:

    -Filter by the collection

    -Item details/preplay screen

    -Viewing the library “By Collection”


    Filter by Collection


    Filtering by collection leaves out all other items in your media list except the items in the collection. Note this is not the “preplay” screen described below.




    Item Details/Preplay Screen


    When viewing the details/preplay screen for an item that belongs to a collection, the entire collection may be shown as “related” information on the screen. This is only applicable for Movie and TV libraries.



    Viewing the Library “By Collection”


    Only show collections that exist in the library. Opening a collection goes to the “collection preplay” screen (see below).





    Collections Preplay


    The collections preplay contains all the items in that library that belong to the collection. It can also include items from other libraries that are in a collection with the exact same name. By adding items in different libraries to identically-named collections, you can relate them to each other. For instance, you can have Star Wars movies, TV shows, and music albums all in a “Star Wars” collection so they’re related to each other.

  • Troubleshooting

  • I get an error message 'Server Unavailable'?

    If you are having difficulty connecting to the server but can access Netphilix.ie then try logging out and then back in again. If that does not solve your problem then contact me at admin@netphilix.ie as the server may be down.

  • I get an error message 'No Media Found'?

    If you are recieving the 'no Media Found' error then you will need to contact me at admin@netphilix.ie, if it is a new account I may have simply forgotten to give you acces to the libraries. If you had access before then I may have been working on the server and not corrected it.

  • My stream is buffering A LOT!

    If your stream is buffering, there could be multiple problems (as listed below), contact me at admin@netphilix.ie and I'll try to help, although please note many of these problems can't be fixed.

    1: Your internet is slow and cannot receive the stream fast enough.
    2: The server's internet is slow and cannot send the stream fast enough.
    3: There may be multiple people streaming from the server causing the internet to max out.
    4: The server may be updating files or installing updates (I try to keep these to AM times only).
    5: The files may be corrupt or incompatible.
    6: Some other weird voodoo shit.

  • I found a Movie/Episode that isn't playing correctly?

    If you have found a movie or episode that is stuttering, audio de-synced, terrible quality, or any other bizzare happenings, here is what to do. First try other movies/episodes to test if it is due to your connection. If it was only that movie/episode that had the problem then send the movie title or the episode's show title, season number and episode number to admin@netphilix.ie, so that I can check the file and redownload a new version if needed.

  • I have more questions...

    If you have any burning questions that have not been answered here then send me a message at admin@netphilix.ie, and I'll get back to you asap!

  • About the server

    Well... It's based on an application called 'Plex Media Server', that's what I use to display everything nicely including the posters, cast, trailers. All of that is done with Plex. I then use 2 different applications to keep the TVShows and Movies downloaded and up to date. They're called Sonarr (TV) and Radarr (Movies).

    Sonarr creates a release calendar for itself so that it can search torrent sites (more on that later) for new episodes of TV Shows that I want to keep updated. Sonarr can also download past episodes that I missed, but it's not reliable in any capacity. Sonarr can also download the video quality of your choice. I have it set to download 720p videos but if none can be found in that quality it'll download them in 1080p, if they're available. Once it finds the episode it sends the link to BitTorrent and then downloads and organises it into the correct show and season folders, and renames the file so Plex can find the file and understand what episode it is.

    Radarr allows me to request movies. It'll wait until the movies been digitally released to start searching the torrent sites. Just like Sonarr, Radarr can download movies in my selected quality, I have it set to search for 1080p videos but if they're unavailable then it'll search for 720p. It can download movies that have been previously released, but it can be very hit and miss depending on how popular the movies is and how long ago it was released. Once it finds the movie, it sends it onto BitTorrent, downloads it, renames it, and organises it into the correct folder for Plex.

    I use another application called Jackett. Sonarr and Radarr have some torrent sites that they can search but it's not reliable or maintained. Jackett can search ALL the sites. They just need to be configured and off you pop.

    The website is run off a NGINX server. This basically sees that someone wishes to connect to the server and depending on the url (e.g. /help, /about, /plex ...) it will route the user to the correct page.
